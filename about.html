ME

I am Jiangtao Kong, also known as Johnson, a fifth-year Computer Science Ph.D. student at William & Mary advised by Prof. Huajie Shao. My research centers on continual learning, world models, and world-model-based online reinforcement learning.

Outside research, I keep a small room in my life for art. I began learning violin when I was three. On days when thoughts knot together, Mozart makes the room quiet again, and Paganini gives the sharper feelings a place to burn. I am drawn to mountains, roads, cities, and the changing light of unfamiliar places. I hope to keep walking across this planet I call home, camera in hand, leaving small records of the places that have held my footsteps. If photography speaks to you too, start with my gallery. I also find joy in beatboxing, popping, streetball, snowboarding, racing, and cooking.
