Research

My research centers on continual learning: enabling AI systems to learn from non-stationary streams of data, tasks, and modalities while retaining previously acquired knowledge. I study this problem across classical computer vision models, vision-language models, world models, and world-model-based online reinforcement learning.

Continual Learning Multimodal Models World Models World-Model-Based Online RL

Research Themes

Continual Learning

I study continual learning for models trained on non-stationary data streams, where new classes, tasks, modalities, or distributions arrive over time. The goal is to learn new knowledge while mitigating catastrophic forgetting under memory and compute constraints.

  • Class-incremental and exemplar-free learning
  • Prototype-based representation consolidation
  • Knowledge retention, forward transfer, and replay
Related Work
ECA · ICML 2026 YoooP · TMLR 2025 Hybrid Memory Replay · arXiv

World Models

I study world models as predictive representations of environment dynamics, especially trajectory-level models that generalize across systems. The goal is to support structured dynamics prediction, planning, control, and world-model-based online reinforcement learning.

  • Knowledge-encoded trajectory world models
  • System-aware dynamics prediction
  • Planning and policy learning with learned models
Related Work
WestWorld · ICML 2026 Spotlight

Continual Alignment for LLMs / MLLMs

I study continual alignment and adaptation for pretrained LLMs and MLLMs under evolving tasks, distributions, and preferences. The goal is to update model behavior while preserving pretrained capabilities, cross-modal alignment, and general instruction-following ability.

  • Continual alignment for pretrained MLLMs
  • Capability preservation under distribution shift
  • Activation steering and preference alignment

Research Arc

2018 - 2020

Classical Computer Vision

I began with classical computer vision problems such as vehicle re-identification and human activity recognition, focusing on visual representation learning, metric learning, and spatiotemporal modeling.

2023 - Present

Continual Learning

My research then moved toward continual learning, studying how models retain knowledge and acquire new capabilities from sequential tasks, classes, and distributions under memory and compute constraints.

2024 - Present

MLLM / LLM Alignment

I extended this continual learning perspective to pretrained MLLMs and LLMs, where adaptation must preserve pretrained capabilities, cross-modal alignment, and general instruction-following behavior.

2025 - Present

World Models

My recent work studies world models as structured predictive representations of environment dynamics, moving from static perception toward trajectory prediction, planning, and control.

2026 - Present

World-Model-Based Online RL

I am now interested in how learned world models can support online reinforcement learning, where predictive models, planning procedures, and policies improve from newly collected trajectories.
